How to Make Salmon

Description

To cook salmon in a pan, start by patting the fillets dry and seasoning them with salt, pepper, and a squeeze of lemon.
Heat a nonstick or well-oiled skillet over medium-high heat. Place the salmon skin-side down and cook for about 4–5 minutes,
pressing gently so the skin gets crispy. Flip and cook the other side for 2–3 minutes until just cooked through.
Finish with a knob of butter and fresh herbs if desired, then serve immediately.

Ingredients

  • 2 salmon fillets (skin-on, about 6 oz each)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Black pepper (to taste)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil or neutral cooking oil
  • 1 tbsp butter (optional)
  • A few sprigs of fresh herbs (like thyme or parsley, optional)
  • 1 garlic clove, crushed (optional)
  • Lemon wedges (for serving)

Steps

  1. Pat salmon dry and season with salt and pepper
  2. Heat oil in a skillet over medium-high heat
  3. Place salmon skin-side down and press gently
  4. Cook for 4 to 5 minutes
  5. Flip and cook for 2 to 3 minutes
  6. Add butter, garlic, and herbs (optional)
  7. Spoon butter over salmon
  8. Remove from pan and rest briefly
  9. Serve with lemon wedges
